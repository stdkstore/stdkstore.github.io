Relativity is Irrelevant

— a survival game about not having a reason to survive

Transmission

i don't know why i'm still doing this


they might still be out there.
that's the only thought that gets me moving.

i walk the same broken streets. i eat whatever i find before my stomach gives out. at night my legs stop working right and everything slows and i just... drag myself forward because stopping feels like a decision i can't take back.

the radiation shifts every morning. some days i can skirt the edges. other days it's so thick i feel it in my teeth. i go in anyway. i always go in.

About

you scavenge food, manage sanity, read the radiation forecast each morning and decide how much of yourself you're willing to spend today. sanity drops and the world starts lying to you.

when you die the map remembers a little. you don't really start over. you just continue differently.

there's no win screen. just a number of days, and at the end — a quiet list of what you did to other people to get there.

2D top-down, PSX-style visuals
day/night cycle, dynamic radiation zones
sanity system that distorts the world
radio towers that only work on good days
multiple endings depending on what you find and who you became
your choices are counted. silently. the whole time.
optimized 2D performance in Godot
