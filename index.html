03 // SYSTEMS

Core Mechanics

  • The radiation doesn't wait. It pushes east — through Zervograd and beyond — and the zones you survive today are tomorrow's dead zones. You are always moving west or you are dying.
  • Day/night cycle with dynamic radiation zones — read the forecast, then decide how far to go.
  • The map expands endlessly east. Better loot in contaminated zones. Higher cost in rads. Everything is a trade.
  • Sanity system that distorts the world the further you fall — hallucinations, false signals, wrong directions.
  • Radio towers — functional only on clear days. Sometimes you hear voices that shouldn't be there.
  • Trade with survivors still clinging to the zones behind you. Help them. Use them. Leave them. It counts.
  • Multiple endings shaped by what you carried, who you were, and what you chose to leave behind.
  • Your choices are counted. Silently. The whole time.
  • PSX-style 2D visuals — built in Godot. No monsters. Just consequence.

05 // DEVELOPER

The Studio

STDK. Solo. Hobby. Godot.

I'm 17, working a mechanical job, building games on the side. School didn't stick. The path toward becoming a "real" developer closed a while ago, and that's just how it is. But I never really stopped making things.

I build games in the gaps — after shifts, on weekends, whenever I've got enough energy left. No team, no budget, no plan to go full-time. Just Godot, a folder full of ideas, and enough stubbornness to finish what I start.

"Not everyone who builds things ends up where they thought they were going. Sometimes you just keep building anyway."
